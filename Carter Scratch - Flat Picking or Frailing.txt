1. Hellen Carter - Bury Me Under The Weeping Willow

https://youtu.be/6yrAMCZYS5I?t=690

"One of the very first sessions that the Carter family recorded in Bristol, Tennesse in August of 1927" (Helen Carter) 

This is the abbridged song played by Helen to Illustrate how her mother played. Some of Maybelle's original is included also for comparison. In the original the chorus follows each verse and is repeated at the end.

The intro has a more complex base line. Once she begins singing she sticks to a regular pattern of alternating base note and scratch (Down stroke with the fingers, sometimes preceded by a lighter up stroke). Instead of Boom--chik-ka (Cash/Perkins), it's boom--chik, boom-ka-chik-. But the original sounds like boom--chik-ka boom--chik-ka.

3. Canonball Blues (He's Solid Gone)

https://youtu.be/6yrAMCZYS5I?t=954

Helen credits "an old black man", [Esley (Lesley) Riddle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesley_Riddle) as showing her how to play the blues. (In fact he was only 4 years older than her mother--Helen probably only heard of him as she was born in '27' the same year she speaks of one of the earliest Carter recording sessions.)
