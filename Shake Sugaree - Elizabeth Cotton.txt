2. Shake Sugaree – Elizabeth Cotton (1967)

https://youtu.be/BO-8NQdzA4o

Elizabeth played left handed upside down. Original by Elizabeth was recorded in the key of B♭ with her 12 year old great-grandaughter singing and released in 1967. There's a video of her playing it in D using C-chords with a capo on 2. I can't find any videos of the way she played it in B♭, but a tab on UG (by flatwound) has the intro correct using A-chords with a capo on 1. 

Note that she uses the same walkdowns and ups Johnny Cash made very distinctive in "I Walk the Line", developed from the Carter Scratch style (which had a great contribution from Leslie Riddle and the songs they heard locally). Elizabeth's earliest recording of any of her work is of the same year as Walk the Line, 1956.
