Next line the G is the same except the last triplet starts on the second string just like earlier in the measure (that's very variable to add some minor difference) C is the same. Basically except for the intro Em, it's a base note followed by two triplets. The first a-m-i is often compressed to ~-am-i (almost always on the first of the double measures). The second is either a simple a-m-i on the high strings or a-P-i or m-P-i. The hammer-on in the C-chord is an exception where the 0h2 is thrown in what is usually a rest between triplets.
